Kyoto

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

A mesmerizing forest of towering bamboo stalks, creating an ethereal and enchanting atmosphere. Visitors can stroll through a path that winds through the towering bamboo, offering a unique and immersive experience in the midst of nature.

Kinkaku-ji Temple

Kinkaku-ji, also known as the Golden Pavilion, is a stunning Zen Buddhist temple located in Kyoto, Japan, renowned for its breathtaking gold leaf-covered exterior and picturesque surroundings. The temple's reflective golden facade, set against a backdrop of meticulously manicured gardens and a tranquil pond, makes it a symbol of beauty and serenity in Japanese architecture.

Potential Lunch Options in Kyoto

Okonomiyaki Katsu

Compact restaurant with griddle tables for cook-your-own pancakes with savory fillings.

Kyogoku Kaneyo

Decades-old institution known for kinshidon bowls with omelet & charcoal-grilled eel over rice.

Hatoya Ryoyousha

One of the best Japanese matcha desserts in Kyoto. Well known for matcha, lattes, flan, and sweet dessert. The shop is small so plan for long waits. Open 11am to 5pm.

Directions

Kimukatsu

Reservations at 5 pm. Place is a bit difficult to find as it's inside a bigger building with a bunch of different shops on multiple floors. Well known for the katsu pork, rice, and miso soup.
