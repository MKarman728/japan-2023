Kamikochi to Takayama

Takayama (高山) is a city in the mountainous Hida region of Gifu Prefecture. To differentiate it from other places named Takayama, the city is also commonly referred to as Hida-Takayama. Takayama retains a traditional touch like few other Japanese cities, especially in its beautifully preserved old town. I

Miyagawa Market

The market is located on either bank of the Miya River between two bridges—Kajibashi and Yayoibashi. You can buy everything from local fruit and vegetables to traditional "sarubobo" dolls and stylish chopsticks.

Shirakawago

Shirakawa is a village located in Ōno District, Gifu Prefecture, Japan. It is best known for being the site of Shirakawa-gō, a small, traditional village showcasing a building style known as gasshō-zukuri.

Shirakawago Three houses

The Historic Villages of Shirakawa-gō and Gokayama are one of Japan's UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The three houses is one of the most famous aspects of Shirakago.

Kanazawa

Take bus for the direction of Kanazawa. Kanazawa is a captivating city located on the coast of the Sea of Japan, renowned for its rich cultural heritage and well-preserved Edo-period districts. It's celebrated for its picturesque Kenrokuen Garden, considered one of Japan's top gardens, and its historic geisha and samurai districts, offering a glimpse into traditional Japanese life. The city also boasts a vibrant arts scene, exquisite regional cuisine, and a blend of modern and historic architecture.

Hyatt House

The Hyatt House in Kanazawa is a luxurious hotel offering upscale accommodation in the heart of the city, providing a blend of modern elegance and comfort. Guests can enjoy spacious rooms, impeccable service, and convenient access to Kanazawa's cultural attractions and culinary delights.
